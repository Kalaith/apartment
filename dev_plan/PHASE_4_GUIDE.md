# Phase 4: Depth & Mastery Roadmap

## Overview
Phase 4 focuses on transitioning the player from a passive observer to an active, strategic manager. This phase is split into four distinct stages of implementation.

**Theme**: The weight of management and the art of the deal.

### [Phase 4A: Economic Realism](file:///h:/WebHatchery/games/apartment/dev_plan/16_ECONOMIC_REALISM.md)
**Focus**: Ongoing costs, taxes, utilities, and critical failures.
- *Removes:* The "Too much money" problem.
- *Adds:* Staffing and financial friction.

### [Phase 4B: Tenant Lifecycle & Acquisition](file:///h:/WebHatchery/games/apartment/dev_plan/17_TENANT_ACQUISITION.md)
**Focus**: Marketing, vetting, and lease negotiations.
- *Removes:* Waiting for random applications.
- *Adds:* Player agency in tenant selection.

### [Phase 4C: Interaction & Relationships](file:///h:/WebHatchery/games/apartment/dev_plan/18_TENANT_INTERACTION.md)
**Focus**: Dialogue, conflict resolution, and community.
- *Removes:* Disconnection from tenants.
- *Adds:* Emotional stakes and choices.

### [Phase 4D: Tutorial & Narrative](file:///h:/WebHatchery/games/apartment/dev_plan/19_TUTORIAL_NARRATIVE.md)
**Focus**: Mentors, rivals, and scripted onboarding.
- *Removes:* Confusion for new players.
- *Adds:* A "Soul" and overarching story.

---

## Success Metrics for Phase 4
- [ ] Every dollar spent feels like a strategic choice.
- [ ] Players plan their building "character" (e.g., "Student Housing" vs. "Luxury Suites").
- [ ] Losing a tenant feels like losing a character, not just a revenue stream.
- [ ] New players can reach the first "Multi-Building" milestone without feeling lost.
